High Performance Computing (HPC) refers to the use of powerful computing systems and parallel processing techniques to solve complex computational problems at high speed. Instead of relying on a single computer, HPC systems use clusters of interconnected computers (called nodes) working together as a single system.

1. **Compute Nodes**

   * Individual machines with CPUs/GPUs
   * Perform actual computation

2. **Interconnect Network**

   * High-speed communication (InfiniBand, Ethernet)
   * Transfers data between nodes

3. **Storage Systems**

   * Parallel file systems (Lustre, GPFS)
   * Designed for high throughput

4. **Job Scheduler**

   * Allocates resources (SLURM, PBS)
   * Manages job execution

5. **Accelerators**

   * GPUs, TPUs
   * Speed up specific computations

HPC plays a critical role in solving problems that are impossible or impractical for standard computers. Many modern challenges involve enormous datasets or require simulations that demand massive computational power.

### ğŸ”¥ Key Reasons HPC Matters

#### 1. Speed and Efficiency

* Used in finance, automotive, aerospace, healthcare, and tech industries
